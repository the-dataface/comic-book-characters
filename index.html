Overweight Men and Underweight Women In Comic Books

Societal Body Types Preferences, Illustrated by Comic Book Characters

Captain America is overweight. At a height of 6’1”, he weighs in at a hefty 240 pounds - 50 pounds heavier than expected for someone of that height. But this doesn’t mean the First Avenger needs to go on a diet - it means he’s the epitome of what society considers the ideal male body type.

It’s no secret film casting favors muscular actors. Or that magazines glorify ultra-thin women. Slim, petite women and muscular, broad men take center stage in all types of popular culture. But comic book creators can take it one step further - they illustrate their characters, designing them in whatever image they please. How these fictional characters are drawn may speak volumes to societal body type preferences.

Do the characters in Marvel and DC universes follow patterns we see in film, magazines, and advertising? Who’s the most represented in this pop culture juggernaut?
